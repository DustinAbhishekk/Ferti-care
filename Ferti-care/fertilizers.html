Fertilizer FAQs

Common questions about our fertilizer products

How often should I fertilize my crops?

The frequency of fertilization depends on the crop type, growth stage, and soil conditions. Most crops benefit from fertilization every 4-6 weeks during the growing season. Always follow the specific recommendations for each fertilizer product.

What's the difference between organic and synthetic fertilizers?

Organic fertilizers are derived from natural sources (plant or animal matter) and release nutrients slowly as they decompose. Synthetic fertilizers are manufactured and provide nutrients in forms that are immediately available to plants. Organic options improve soil health over time, while synthetics offer precise nutrient control.

Can I mix different fertilizers together?

Some fertilizers can be safely mixed, but others may react negatively. Always check compatibility before mixing. As a general rule, don't mix fertilizers with pesticides unless the label specifically allows it. When in doubt, apply products separately.

How should I store fertilizers?

Store fertilizers in a cool, dry place away from moisture and direct sunlight. Keep them in their original containers with lids tightly closed. Store granular and liquid fertilizers separately, and keep all fertilizers away from children, pets, and food products.
